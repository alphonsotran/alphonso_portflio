About Myself

My Story

As a medical researcher who has refactored into a full stack web developer in 2017, I bring a fresh perspective to tech with strong analytical skills, adaptability, and open-mindedness. I decided to pursue tech in order to solve meaningful problems by building rapid, practical solutions with my own hands and a keyboard. My greatest joy is sharing this passion with like-minded individuals and coming up with creative answers to everyday problems.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why did I decide to become a web developer?

I have always been interested in computers growing up. I would build computers and play around with MS-DOS when I was little. As I got older and worked in medical research, I would catch myself tinkering with lab and medical software to "optimize" its use. Overtime, I came to realize that both medicine and programming are not exclusive, so I joined a full-stack web developer bootcamp to transition into the world of tech. I am enjoying where I am in this point of my life. In the future, I hope to merge medicine and tech together.

How long have I been coding?

I've been coding ever since February 2017. My journey has just started!

What is my go-to tech stack?

At the moment, my tech stack includes a broad range of languages and frameworks including JavaScript, Ruby, Solidity, NodeJS, React and React Native, and many others. As a person who is always constantly trying to learn new things, I am currently learning and developing decentralized apps based on Solidity at the moment. I am hopeful that my next chapter in life will involve jumping into the field of Machine Learning and AI.
